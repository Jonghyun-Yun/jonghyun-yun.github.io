HW 1

Show all your work (including R code) to receive full credit.

Question 1

Use Brain data to answer this question.

a)

Calculate mean, median, standard deviation, and IQR of BrainWeight. Which ones are appropriate measures of the center and spread, respectively?

b)

Use an appropriate statistical method to investigate the normality of BrainWeight.

c)

Use the same method in b) to investigate the normality of log(BrainWeight).

Question 2

Use Brain data to answer this question.

a)

Create a scatter plot of BrainWeight and BodyWeight. Calculate the correlation between these two variables.

b)

Create a scatter plot of log(BrainWeight) and log(BodyWeight). Calculate the correlation between these two variables.

c)

Is the correlation coefficient in a) appropriately describing the relationship of two raw variables? why or why not?

Question 3

Use SCF data to answer questions below:

a)

Calculate means of Absorption for each Treatment value.

b)

Investigate the normality of Absorption when Treatment = SCF. Repeat the analysis when Treatment = Control.

c)

If you don’t separate Absorption by Treatment value, does Absorption show the normality?

Question 4

Use Brain data to answer this question.

a)

Perform the least square regression of BrainWeight on BodyWeight. What is \(R^{2}\)? Interpret the regression line you obtained.

b)

Acquire the residual from a) to draw a residual plot (residual vs BodyWeight). Are the residual randomly scattered?

c)

Investigate the normality of the residuals.

Question 5

Generate 10,000 random numbers from the standard normal distribution using set.seed(1); x = rnorm(10000).

a)

Count how may values in x is between -2 and 2.

b)

Calculate the variance of x.

c)

What should be the variance of y = 10 + 5 * x?

Question 6

(Written) The time to complete an exam is approximately Normal with a mean of 70 minutes and a standard deviation of 10 minutes.

a)

What percentage of students will complete the exam in under an hour?

b)

If 25% of students completed the exam after you did it, how many minutes did you spend to complete the exam?
